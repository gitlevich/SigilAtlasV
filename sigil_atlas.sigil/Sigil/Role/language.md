# Role

The position-term under which one @POV's @self holds another @sigil. Local to that @self, drawn from a role-taxonomy, distinct from the held @sigil's globally-unique @Name.

A @sigil has one @Name; the same @sigil has potentially many @Roles, one per @self that has named it. My @Name is Vlad in all attention; in my mama's @self I occupy a @Role from her family-relation taxonomy (`сын`), and in my @self she occupies a reciprocal @Role from mine (`мама`). One edge, two lexical projections.

A role-taxonomy is itself a @sigil — authorable or importable — supplying the vocabulary of role-terms along with their reciprocal pairings. A family taxonomy pairs `mother` with `child`, `father` with `child`, `sister` with `sister`. A professional taxonomy pairs `employer` with `employee`, `colleague` with `colleague`. An idiosyncratic taxonomy coins its own. When a @Role is installed by @Naming, the language looks up its reciprocal and installs the matching edge on the other side; if none is declared, the other side holds a generic @Neighbor - edge until that side names it.

@Roles are multi-valued. A @sigil entering my @self as `мама` can also enter as `first-reader`; each @Naming call adds an edge. The set of @Roles under which a @sigil enters my @self is how I hold it. The intersection across many @selfs is the shape of its social presence.

On the Inside canvas of my @self, the door-glyph to a @Neighbor is labeled by the @Role — not by the @Name — because the door is local to my @self. Walking through enters that @sigil, where the door back to me is labeled by its @Role for me. Same edge, two labels.
