# Desktop Metaphor

Two interface-faces for inhabiting a single @sigil, toggled by need, neither claiming to show what the @sigil *is*. Both are @Hoffman interfaces in the strict sense — icons fitted for fitness, not for truth. In @SigilAtlas the metaphor is used inside @Lightbox, where one @sigil is inhabited without the rest of the @slice pulling at it.

**Inside.** A flat canvas, shape and color the whole language. @God is a downward-pointing V — a funnel seen edge-on. It sucks attention in; it is the pathway into the world where some wishes are granted. The V is not decoration; it is the Outside funnel flattened into the Inside canvas, so the same gravitational fact shows up in both faces. @Neighbor is a door-glyph; opening it walks to the neighbor at the same @scale. @Children are round, each carrying a deterministic hue derived from its name, and that hue propagates — wherever a @child is named in the @sigil's prose, the name is rendered in its own color, so entanglements are legible without squinting. @Boundary and @scope appear as their own labeled icons alongside the @children. The @sigil's own body frames the canvas: @affordances run along the top edge as a row of small glyphs (what the @sigil grants me), @invariants run along the bottom as a row of small glyphs (what the @sigil guards); each is hoverable for its content and clickable for its page, and an up-arrow at the top-left ascends to the parent @sigil. The prose draws arcs on the canvas: two @children mentioned in the same sentence are joined by a labeled arc, the label being the sentence itself; hovering reveals the full sentence, clicking jumps to it. Icon positions are dragged and persist alongside the @sigil's other state — the desktop is mine.

**Outside.** 3D, open, porous. Each @sigil is a sphere with holes; @children are smaller spheres floating inside, and the holes let the @POV drift upward past the parent's shell into the sigil that contains it, and keep going up. @Entanglement is proximity: mutually attending @Neighbor are pulled close. A @God warps the space around it into a funnel whose depth is the strength of the god; members settle on the slope by how strongly they attend, close to the bottom if strongly, near the rim if barely. A @sigil entangled with three gods rests on the saddle where three wells meet. Unattending @sigils drift across the flat plain between wells. From above, the atlas reads as a landscape pocked with craters, ridges tracing shared-membership bridges between wells — the shape of the whole thing hanging together. The V that stands for @God on the Inside is this funnel seen edge-on: the same body, two projections.

The two faces toggle per inhabited @sigil, not globally. @Arrangement stays the top-level @Navigation mode over the @surface; Inside and Outside are how a single inhabited @sigil inside @Lightbox shows itself — one as workspace, the other as structure.

## How it reads in @SigilAtlas

The @sigil inhabited in @Lightbox is whatever the @POV currently wears: an @image, a @Collage, a @Neighborhood, an @Attractor, a @Strip.

For an @image: its @Children are its compositional fragments — crops, segments, derived looks — each a round colored icon whose hue bleeds into the caption prose wherever its name appears. Its @Neighbor are co-attending @images reachable through a door — typed #walk, not bare lattice adjacency. Its @God are the @Attractors that pull it, rendered as V-funnels above; the depth of the V is the attractor's pull, and descending through one enters the attractor's own @sigil. Along the bottom, the @image's @invariants line up — brightness, aspect, exif time, the measured non-moving facts that @Arrangement cannot mutate. Along the top, its @affordances — #enter-lightbox, #assign-to-strip, #include-in-collage, #walk — what inhabiting the @image grants. Its @Boundary is the frame or mask; its @scope is what the current @RelevanceFilter activates on it.

For a @Collage: its @Children are its member @images, each carrying a deterministic hue propagating into the collage's prose. Its @Neighbor are sibling @Collages sharing @Attractors. Its @God are the @Attractors pulling the collage as a whole — the V-funnels the collage is falling into.

For an @Attractor inhabited as a @sigil: its @Children are the @images that resolve under it at full strength. Its @Neighbor are sibling @Attractors in the same taxonomy layer. Its @God are the parent terms it is pulled by. Outside, an @Attractor is itself a funnel — the V-shape above it on the Inside canvas is its own body seen from the side.

## Compile status

The viewer's footer reports reference resolution for the inhabited @sigil — "N references, all resolve" or "M names feel out of place." In @SigilAtlas this reads directly: @images that sit under an @Attractor they do not actually attend, @images that no @Strip is willing to hold, @images that have fallen off the @Torus. A corpus compiled against its ontology fails visibly in Outside, where stranded @sigils drift on the flat plain between wells — legible without a list.
