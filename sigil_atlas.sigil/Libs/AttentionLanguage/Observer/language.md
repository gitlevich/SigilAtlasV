# Observer

@Attention paid from a point of view at a @scale. Attends to the @frame (space-like). Registers @observations along @contrasts.

The observer has two tuning knobs that shape what a thrown @sigil admits. Selectivity is how many @contrasts are currently relevant — the cardinality of active dimensions. More selective = more constraints, smaller admitted region, narrower projection. Discrimination is how narrow the preferred range along each relevant contrast — the tightness of each `Discriminate`. Narrower = deeper pull, sharper feathering. Both are bounded by @Resolution: past the @SpatialResolution ceiling the observer cannot distinguish further, so additional selectivity or discrimination is wasted @attention.
