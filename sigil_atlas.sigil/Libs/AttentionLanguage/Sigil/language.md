# Sigil

A recursive structural element with the property of capturing @POVs. A @POV is said to inhabit or wear a @sigil.

A sigil has a @boundary that provides the inhabitant with @affordances and protects with @invariants. Inhabiting a sigil activates its @scope — @contrasts, @things, and @attractors named inside resolve within it. Sigils nest: a sigil is contained inside its parent, surrounded by peers at its own @scale, and contains its @children.

The first-person vocabulary — what a sigil is from the point of view of someone inhabiting one — lives in @POV. @POV names @Self (the sigil I identify with) and the positions other sigils occupy relative to @Self (@Children, @Neighbor, @God, @World). Sigil-the-concept is the building block; @POV is the inhabitant whose inhabiting gives the block its relations.

## Metaphors

**Door**: A @sigil is like a door: it affords entry into an unbounded world of another. A door can be named and recognized.

**Predicate + Functor**: the @invariants work like a predicate (what must hold inside), and the @affordances as functions on the values expressed in terms of @contrasts the @sigil deems relevant.
