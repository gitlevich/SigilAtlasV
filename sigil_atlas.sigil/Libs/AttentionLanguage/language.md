# Attention Language

The mechanics of @attention as experienced from a first-person point of view at a given @scale. Both @timelike and @spacelike.

Here the exact semantics are lexical: @sigils are nested scopes over domain language. Imported @DifferentialGeometry gives names to the shape of those scopes in experience — simultaneous structure, sequential articulation, reachability, and projection.

The center is @POV — me, the point of view, the "I am" that persists under all transformation of scope. @Attention is what I spend; @FixedPoint is where I radiate from; @Resolution is how finely I can distinguish. I attend in two modes: @spacelike (ambient, whole-frame, at rest) and @timelike (directional, sequential, in motion). @Agent is me in @timelike — me wearing a @Self @sigil and projecting a @narrative to keep it coherent across @frames.

I @collapse @observations one @frame at a time into a @narrative — compressing @spacelike experience into a @timelike trajectory. The @resolution of the collapse is bounded by finite @attention.

I inhabit a @Self that protects my attention by defining the region of @contrastSpace where @invariants about @contrasts of @relevance to me hold. From inside @Self, other @sigils occupy positions: @Children inside, @Neighbor around, @God worshipped above by many, @World given structurally above all. These and @Self are named under @POV because they are first-person positions relative to me.

@SigilEngineering is the practice of converting lived experience to @sigils.
