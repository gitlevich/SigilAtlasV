# Lighting

The illumination of the @surface. Attention is light — moving a light over the terrain is literally the act of attending to it. Two modes, mirroring the two shapes of attention.

Directional lighting is @narrative. A beam — sweeping, sequential, bound by a path. One ridge illuminated at a time, the rest in shadow. The user dragging a point or directional light is performing left-hemisphere analytic attention: this region, now this one, now this. Casts shadows, reveals curvature, makes the terrain readable as a sequence of revealings.

Ambient lighting is @spacelike. The whole field illuminated simultaneously, no direction, no path, no traversal. The sigil fully lit is the sigil fully grasped — right-hemisphere gestalt, the structure apprehended at once. No shadows, no parallax-from-light; every cell contributes equally to the picture.

The two modes are not mutually exclusive. Most real viewing mixes them: an ambient base plus one or more directional lights, each directional light carrying its own color and angle. Different lights reveal different structure — warm light warms the warm neighborhoods, cool light reveals the cool ones. A @contrast between two lights is itself a comparison tool.

Lighting is only meaningful when the @surface has @Relief. On a flat surface, there is nothing for a light to reveal — surface normals are uniform and every direction is equivalent.
