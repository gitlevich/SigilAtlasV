# Compose

A three-pane view where I author my @sigil by driving one pane at a time and watching the others conform. Modeled on a non-linear video editor: a @Source pane on the top left (the @corpus as available material), a @Program pane on the top right (the composed @sigil rendered @spacelike — the standing-wave of whatever I've thrown), and a @Timeline pane along the bottom (the current @sequencing as @timelike, with a playhead). The three are projections of the same self-@sigil kept in sync.

One pane is the driver at any moment; the others conform. I #drive-source when I want to reach into the corpus and throw something new into the composition. I #drive-program when I want to rearrange what I've already thrown — add contrasts, narrow preferences, nudge attractors. I #drive-timeline when I want to walk the narrative, advance the playhead, reorder the sequence. I #switch-driver explicitly; the active pane is visibly marked.

@Attention is light, and light has a finite budget. Driving the @Timeline points the beam into the @narrative — the directional light in @Lighting — and the @Program's ambient picks up the tonal aggregate of what the playhead is touching. A dark narrative literally darkens the Program; a luminous one brightens it. !light-follows-attention.

Compose is the authoring mode; @SpaceLike alone and @TimeLike alone are the inhabiting modes. When I am composing I am an @agent with both modes in hand at once; when I am inhabiting I am an @observer at rest in one of them. @Explore lets me #change-mode into Compose from either inhabiting mode and back out.

!one-driver. !passives-conform. !light-follows-attention.
