# Relevance Filter

A narrative operator written in @SigilML. It defines the @invariants of the @slice — the predicate every @image must satisfy to survive into the @slice. Boolean composition over atoms — @things, @contrast ranges, @TargetImages — joined by n-ary AND and OR and unary NOT over peer-groups.

Two faces of one sigil. Used as a #query affordance, I actively ask for @images matching it. Used as an invariant, it declares what must hold inside the @slice.

Its role is not subsetting. The @corpus is a spacelike field — all @images coexist in @contrastspace. The @RelevanceFilter is the gate that compresses that field into what is worth attending to. In @TimeLike mode it becomes the bandpass of a spectral projection; in @SpaceLike mode it is the gate that shapes which @images are present in the field at all. Same expression, different role in the surrounding transform.

Changing the filter is not filtering a database — it is rewriting a narrative operator. The @slice is the result of evaluating it against the @corpus.
