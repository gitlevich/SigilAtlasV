# Space-like

In this mode I make sense of the @images spatially, as a whole. The arrangement is the @spacelike shape of attention: the field is apprehended all at once, not traversed. @images are center-cropped to squares and tile the @torus in a contiguous field with no gaps.

Each @image has a target position computed as the equilibrium of gravitational pulls from the active @attractors, with @contrasts shaping the @proximity metric. Targets are then assigned to square cells on the @torus so the field stays contiguous. A @neighborhood forms around each @attractor; neighborhoods overlap smoothly where pulls are comparable, and their superposition forms a standing-wave pattern — itself a @sigil, the self the @observer currently inhabits.

Each thrown @sigil projects onto the @surface as a sphere centered at its @FixedPoint in @ContrastSpace. The projected footprint has three derived parameters: richness of the sigil's taxonomy governs diameter (a rich taxonomy needs surface area to unfold its internal terraces); the force of the throw governs depth (gravity); `Discriminate` narrowness governs feathering of the boundary. A crude sigil is a narrow, sharp-edged well; a rich taxonomy thrown softly is a wide, shallow basin with internal terraces; a rich taxonomy thrown hard is a wide, deep basin.

The richness that manifests at any given moment is not the taxonomy's intrinsic depth but how much of it has been articulated. See @Arrangement for the @TimeLike/@SpaceLike duality: @SpaceLike richness is the superposition of articulated root-to-leaf paths, and articulation depth is bounded by @SpatialResolution at the current viewport. Zoom raises the local resolution and admits deeper articulation under the lens.

Cells tile the @torus contiguously with no gaps. Interference between thrown @sigils modulates density and ranking — it does not create emptiness. A genuine hole appears only when `Not` exhausts the local admittance (the repelled set covers the slice under this region) or when a void-sigil is thrown explicitly; both are declared acts of the @observer, not emergent artifacts of superposition.

Every change to a @control — discriminate-range drag, added @contrast, new @attractor, different @visionModel — recomputes targets, re-assigns cells, and animates each @image from its old cell to its new one. No snap; the surface flows like a gravitational field settling.

To shape the arrangement, I #define-controls to manipulate @attractors and @contrasts. The @arrangement is itself a @SigilML expression: each @attractor is an `Attract` node over an atom (a @thing or a @TargetImage); the @RelevanceFilter is the enclosing gate. Ordering is the business of @TimeLike.
