# Neighborhood

The region of the @torus surrounding an @attractor, where @images near it by @proximity congregate. The @attractor sits at the center — for a @thing, the most representative @image; for a @TargetImage, itself. @images arrange by decreasing @proximity outward.

In @spacelike, @images are center-cropped to squares and tile the surface contiguously with no gaps. When multiple @attractors are active, their neighborhoods overlap smoothly: an @image pulled by several @attractors settles where the combined gravitational pull balances. The surface is a continuous field, no disjoint patches, no hard boundaries.

Distinctness of neighborhoods comes from the @slice's @relevanceFilter, not from spatial separation.

The @POV determines which neighborhood I am in. Zooming out reveals the neighborhood structure. Zooming in enters one neighborhood and shows individual @images.
