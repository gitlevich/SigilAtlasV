# Time-like

The @torus arranged from the current @sequencing as a continuous timeline. The arrangement is the @timelike shape of attention: @narrative, not field — the @slice is played back frame by frame along one axis. @time flows left-to-right within each @strip and top-to-bottom across strips — like reading a book. The strip wraps: the end meets the beginning. I can spin it — #playback moves through the sequence.

Within a strip, @images are continuous in the active sequence. Adjacent strips are nearby in that same sequence. The @torus flattens to a square so horizontal and vertical distance feel proportional.

@stories are detected from the current @sequencing at the active @timescale. Zooming the timescale regroups images into coarser or finer temporal clusters.

@timelike is a rendering of sequence, not a definition of the query. It is a @SigilML expression composed of `Bandpass` operators (which @contrast ranges gate the slice) and an `OrderBy` operator (which axis projects the field onto time). The current @relevanceFilter determines which @images are present; `OrderBy` determines their sequence. Many different @timelike narratives live in the same @spacelike sigil — change the projection axis and you get a different song from the same spectrum.
