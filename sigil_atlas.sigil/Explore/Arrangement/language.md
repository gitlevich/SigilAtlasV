# Arrangement

How @images are laid out on the @surface given the current @slice. Two kinds, distinguished by the shape of attention they support: @SpaceLike arranges by @similarity as an ambient field (all-at-once apprehension); @TimeLike arranges by @sequencing along @time as a narrative (frame-by-frame reading). Each mode is a @SigilML expression that operates on the @slice — SpaceLike admits `Attract` operators over atoms; TimeLike admits `Discriminate` and `OrderBy` operators. Changing the arrangement rewrites the expression; evaluating it produces the layout.

The two modes are duals. A @TimeLike rendering is a 1D projection of the thrown @sigils' taxonomies along one path; a @SpaceLike rendering is the superposition of all such projections. Neither fully exists without the other: a @sigil whose taxonomy has never been traversed cannot manifest its full @SpaceLike shape (the richness it contributes to the standing-wave is proportional to paths already walked), and a @SpaceLike pattern has no way to be narrated except as a sequence of @TimeLike projections through it. @Compose makes both visible at once, under @observer control of which drives which.
