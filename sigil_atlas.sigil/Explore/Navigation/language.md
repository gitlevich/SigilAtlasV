# Navigation

How I move my @PointOfView over the @surface. Panning changes position; zooming changes distance. The @Frame is what I see at the current position and zoom.

Zoom is an act of raising @SpatialResolution in the viewed region. Higher resolution admits more @contrasts simultaneously and permits the articulator to walk thrown taxonomies deeper where the ceiling has risen. Rich @sigils refine their internal terraces in the zoomed region; the local standing-wave gains finer structure. Panning and zooming out do not re-articulate — the coarser view is already contained in what has been walked, and structure below the current ceiling collapses back into its parent terrace.
