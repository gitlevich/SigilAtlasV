# Contrast Control

Lets me define a @contrast by naming two @poles. A good @contrast is a span between mutually exclusive polar opposites, like dark vs bright, or colors on the opposite sides of the Color Wheel.

A @contrast can be primitive — just its two @poles — or compound, holding dropped-in constituent @contrasts in its body that sharpen what the top-level @poles distinguish. Constituents are neighbor @contrasts at the same scale; each #compose goes one level up in abstraction.

Lets me select a preferred range along each @contrast via @Discriminate control.

Defined @contrasts are !persistent, so I accumulate a library over time. I can #pick-from-library to bring a previously defined @contrast back into active use without redefining it.

Visually, it is a compact horizontal slider with @pole names underneath. Enter to activate. X icon to #delete (with confirmation). #rearrange by dragging.

Used to #filter @images.
