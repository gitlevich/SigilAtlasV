# Visual Neighborhoods

Regions of the @torus in @spacelike mode where images look alike. Not an explicit data structure — they emerge from the similarity ordering. When images are sorted by embedding distance and laid into @strips, visually coherent groups naturally cluster on the surface.

A neighborhood has no hard boundary. It is a density: a region where nearby images share visual properties (color palette, texture, composition, subject). Moving across the @torus in @spacelike mode is moving between neighborhoods.

The @pointOfView determines which neighborhood I am in. Zooming out reveals the neighborhood structure — patches of similar color and texture across the surface. Zooming in enters one neighborhood and shows individual images.
