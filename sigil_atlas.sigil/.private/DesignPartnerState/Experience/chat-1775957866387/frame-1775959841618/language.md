Yes. Spacelike now feels like the mode where the fixed frame is a local neighborhood cut from the current slice, and its meaning is simultaneity, co-presence, and comparison within that neighborhood rather than progression along an order or attraction along taste.

So the question to tighten is not “what is shown” in the abstract, but “what counts as one neighborhood in the frame, and what counts as one step with an arrow key.” My instinct is that in Spacelike, an arrow step should mean moving to an adjacent region within the same sliced containment world, while preserving the sense that everything visible is equally present at once and not earlier or later than anything else.
