## Partner

Yes — that explains the feeling almost exactly. The main fracture is that the app has one very strong concrete promise, “help me find my taste in my image corpus by moving through a toroidal visual surface,” while leftover ontology keeps trying to make the app also be an instance of a much more universal theory of attention, sigils, names, and invariants.

So the biggest coherence breaks are where words stop serving the product and start serving the old framework instead. You can feel this in the local “Sigil” subtree, in terms like fixed points and globally unique names, and in places where “RelevanceFilter,” “sigilControls,” “contrastspace,” “time direction,” and “sigil builder” overlap without quite settling into one clean mental model.

The good news is the core is very coherent underneath the leftovers. If you prune or demote the inherited ontology to background reference and let the product language lead, this sigil will probably snap into focus fast.
