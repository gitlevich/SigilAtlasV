@Explore is the focus of the prototype specification and the most creative part of the project, but it remains underdefined at an elemental level: the spec must say, in the user’s language, what appears in the fixed @Frame at a given moment, what the arrow keys and other @NavigationGesture verbs do to that appearance, what state is carried in each of @Timelike, @Spacelike, and @TasteLike, what counts as a @Move, and what remains invariant as one more contrast is re-added. @Explore owns the product interaction within the app layer, intentionally distinct from the reflective ontology layer under @Sigil and @Libs; within that app-layer role, @Torus already sits directly under @Explore as a first-class, primary child and source of spatial-temporal geometric truth, so sibling sigils under @Explore such as @FilterControls, @ExploreViewStateProjection, and @ExplorePreferenceRelevanceDynamics should consume @Torus rather than redefining it. @Explore should be defined as an @InteractionGrammar over recursively sliced, contrast-stratified @ContainmentStructure systems built from @ImageNeighborhoodSigils, rather than as zoom or magnification through content: the corpus is recursively re-formed into multiple @ContainmentStructure views by removing contrasts, and @Explore traverses @Slice paths through those structures. The @Frame keeps the same resolution and aspect ratio, the fully zoomed-out view is pure morphology before image identity, navigation occurs within a viewport structure represented by @Spacelike and grounded in @Torus, and traversal preserves an invariant @Slice through reduced corpus space represented by @Timelike under the @OrientationInvariant. In @Explore, “going deeper” is not revealing hidden detail from one fixed embedding but re-adding one contrast to the sliced sigil structure at each generation, under a recursive promise that every visible cell at generation n maps to a bounded set of child cells at generation n+1 whose union preserves the parent cell’s place in the @Frame; this transition rule refines a @Slice through an @ImageNeighborhoodSigils hierarchy so the same world becomes more discriminating while keeping orientation stable. At the same time, @Explore makes ordering appear to depend on @Mode and layout, creating an explicit tension with @Slice about whether ordering is intrinsic to the traversal or contextual to presentation; the spec should therefore distinguish invariant @Slice identity from @Explore ordering as rendered through @Frame, @Mode, and layout policy. Across @Timelike, @Spacelike, and @TasteLike, the three modes should share the same verbs while interpreting them differently, yielding one coherent interface rather than three separate apps, and @Explore therefore needs missing structural sigils including @Viewpoint, @Focus, @Lens, @NavigationGesture, @PreferenceField, and @Frame, plus an explicit account of @Uncertainty and the tension between exploit and explore so creative discovery retains @ProductiveSurprise rather than collapsing into recommendation sludge. A practical prototype can avoid full recursive sigil neighborhoods by using a @LocalNeighborhoodGraph over images, projection into the @Frame, anchoring on one @ImageCluster or image, and recording @UserFeedbackSignals so ordering can reweight; in this prototype, every frame is a set of images chosen by a @FramePolicy over relevance to current @Focus, novelty relative to what has just been seen, and estimated preference from user feedback.
