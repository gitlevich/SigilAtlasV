@Explore is philosophically specified but still lacks full operational contracts: exactly what state exists, what transforms are allowed, what remains invariant under each transform, and what data structure realizes each visible phenomenon. The next step remains to define @Explore as a state machine over a geometric model, including what the corpus embedding is, what a neighborhood is, what spacelike view returns, what timelike traversal orders, how zoom refines without breaking orientation, how preference updates the field, and which UI variations are explicitly declared non-semantic. Two connected failure modes now sharpen that contract: stale-state reasoning, where the assistant answers from remembered fragments or conceptual fit rather than the actual current @Sigil tree, and surfaced-state visibility failure, where tool results are not reliably carried into conversational state as stable inspectable context across turns. Together these can produce fragment memory, inference, and structurally wrong claims even when @Sigil itself is coherent, especially after external restoration changes state outside the assistant’s direct actions. Therefore the operational contract for @Explore should require explicit fresh reads of @Sigil before structural claims, clear disclosure when current visibility or tool-state continuity is uncertain, and corrective behavior that re-reads and speaks only from actually present observed structure, grounding @Explore’s state-machine semantics in current surfaced state rather than inferred memory.
