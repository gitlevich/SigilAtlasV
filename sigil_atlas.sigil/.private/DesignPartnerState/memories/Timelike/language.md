@Timelike is one of the three modes of @Explore and a native, central part of the app’s geometry and interaction model, alongside @Spacelike, slice, and sigil rather than leftover editor ontology. It is the sliced-plane aspect of the sigil, arising from slicing a plane through a 3D reduction of embedding spaces, and is considered a support mode relative to @TasteLike: in @Timelike, the @Lens privileges an ordering contrast, with narrated traversal through an ordered slice, and @Move advances next along the chosen direction. The user should be able to control the slice’s orientation in 3D space and choose the direction of time: @Timelike selects the traversal direction through the world defined with @Spacelike, effectively choosing which concurrently happening movie-thread to narrate as forward. This makes @Timelike partially overlap semantically with @TimeDirection in screen controls, so their distinction should remain explicit. The underlying slice remains the same as zoom reveals more detail in @Explore, supporting a “watching basically concurrently happening movies” exploration style, correlating the image the user finds beautiful with the cellular shape seen at the fullest zoom, and supporting multi-scale refinement in @Spacelike, where each new zoom generation adds one more contrast.
