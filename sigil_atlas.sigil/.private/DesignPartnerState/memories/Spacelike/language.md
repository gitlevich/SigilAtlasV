@Spacelike is one of the three modes of @Explore and the support mode relative to @TasteLike, and it is central to the product spec rather than a stray leftover editor ontology: together with @Timelike, it defines the native interaction geometry and the world through which exploration happens. In @Explore, @Spacelike is the television-like fixed frame showing a local neighborhood cut from the current slice and carrying the navigation and presentation state for exploration; it defines what counts as a neighborhood, the local neighborhood relation, and simultaneity within the world that @Timelike traverses. Its meaning is co-presence and comparison within that neighborhood, rather than progression along an order or attraction along @TasteLike. In @Spacelike, the @Lens privileges neighborhood relations and simultaneous comparison within a local manifold; motion pans, zooms, and re-centers within the same sliced containment world, and arrow-key navigation should mean moving one step to an adjacent region within that same world while preserving the sense that everything visible is equally present at once, not earlier or later than anything else. As a @Move, advance means going deeper into the selected neighborhood scale. @Spacelike presents structures within structures within structures across many scales; at full zoom-out it should reduce an enormous corpus to a black-and-white cellular structure with white cells separated by black boundaries. Formally, @Spacelike is defined by a slice through reduced embedding space, with the slice orientation controlled by @Timelike: @Spacelike defines the local world structure, while @Timelike chooses traversal direction through that same world.
