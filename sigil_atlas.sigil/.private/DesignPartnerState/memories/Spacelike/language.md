@Spacelike is one of the three modes of @Explore and the support mode relative to @TasteLike: a television-like fixed frame showing a local neighborhood cut from the current slice, carrying the navigation and presentation state for exploration in @Explore. Its meaning is simultaneity, co-presence, and comparison within that neighborhood, rather than progression along an order or attraction along @TasteLike. In @Spacelike, the @Lens privileges neighborhood relations and simultaneous comparison within a local manifold; motion pans, zooms, and re-centers within the same sliced containment world, and arrow-key navigation should mean moving one step to an adjacent region within that world while preserving the sense that everything visible is equally present at once, not earlier or later than anything else. The main design question to tighten is what exactly counts as one neighborhood in the fixed frame and what counts as one arrow-key step there. As a @Move, advance means going deeper into the selected neighborhood scale. @Spacelike presents structures within structures within structures across many scales; at full zoom-out it should reduce an enormous corpus to a black-and-white cellular structure with white cells separated by black boundaries. Formally, @Spacelike is defined by a slice through reduced embedding space, with the slice orientation controlled by @Timelike.
