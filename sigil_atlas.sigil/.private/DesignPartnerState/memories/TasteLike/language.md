@TasteLike is the real heart of @Explore, while @Timelike and @Spacelike are support modes: it defines a preference-gradient @Lens through which motion follows attraction, surprise, or contrast in the user’s evolving @PreferenceField, and the user’s actions gradually deform that field by teaching the system what “more like this” means. Its felt meaning still remains somewhat open but should stay within the same world as the other modes; a next design step is to specify what exactly is shown in the fixed frame in @TasteLike and what a single arrow-key step means there. In @TasteLike, advance can be interpreted as movement toward regions of higher predicted preference, more concretely as a frontier of candidate images balancing predicted beauty, diversity, and @Uncertainty; moving forward increases confidence in a local taste region, sideways preserves appeal while changing contrasts, and zooming changes the scale of similarity. @TasteLike also serves as an aesthetic consistency criterion across scale: images judged beautiful should correlate with the cellular shape revealed at fullest zoom, it helps control the orientation of @Timelike in reduced 3D embedding space, and it influences what morphology appears in @Spacelike at the coarsest scale.
